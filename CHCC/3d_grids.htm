3D Grids

The animation below attempts to provide a "birds-eye" view of changes that have occurred between 1958 to 1995. The display powerfully conveys the expansion of the westward bowl or embayment (located immediately below the term "Black") from a comparatively small feature in 1958 to the huge scarp that is evident today.

This animation was derived from data mainly generated in the late 1980's using imagery originally acquired in 1958, 1969, 1976, 1988 and using techniques that were "state-of-the-art" at the time. Although dated, this method of presentation remains effective, hence its inclusion. The "lines" are actually a series of profiles cut regularly through the landslide in an east-west direction and at an interval of 20 metres. The birds eye view is then created by viewing these data isometrically in a 3D Computer Aided Design (CAD) system. The method is weakened because no contextural data is contained and the density of surface information is comparatively low. The approach was repeated in 1995 using the epoch acquired in March of that year, but has not been used recently.
